The Ni-Wakati Story
"It's Time" — A Movement Born from Purpose
Our Genesis
Born from a vision to bridge Africa's sports development gap, Ni-Wakati Sports Africa emerged as a response to the untapped potential within communities across the continent. "Ni-Wakati" — Swahili for "It's Time" — reflects our urgent call to action.

We observed a disconnect: while Africa produces world-class athletic talent, grassroots sports infrastructure remains underdeveloped. Our founders — sports professionals, community organizers, and development experts — united around a singular mission: to build inclusive sports ecosystems that serve as catalysts for social transformation.

What began as local community soccer programs in Nairobi's informal settlements has evolved into a pan-African movement. Today, we partner with governments, international brands, and local communities to create sustainable sports development models that prioritize accessibility, innovation, and measurable impact.
Our Compass
🌍 Vision
To see the African community empowered through accessible, innovative sports ecosystems that drive social cohesion, economic opportunity, and continental pride.

🎯 Mission
To partner with communities, institutions, and brands in developing sustainable sports programs that unlock potential, foster talent, and create lasting social impact across Africa.

⚡ The Ni-Wakati Difference
We don't just run events — we build ecosystems. Our approach integrates sports development with tourism, technology, and community enterprise to create self-sustaining models.
Our Guiding Principles
🤝 Community-Led Design
We don't impose solutions — we co-create with communities. Every program begins with listening, understanding local context, and building from existing strengths.

🌱 Sustainable Models
Our programs are designed for longevity. We train local coaches, establish management committees, and create revenue streams to ensure continuity beyond our direct involvement.

🔗 Cross-Sector Integration
We connect sports with tourism, education, health, and technology. A running event becomes a tourism opportunity. A soccer program integrates health education.

📊 Data-Driven Impact
Every program is measured against clear social impact metrics: youth engagement, skills development, health outcomes, and community cohesion indicators.
